Stage 1 Punjabi (Available in 2026)

Soan Chiri School is registered to deliver SACE Stage 1 Punjabi. This program supports students to strengthen Punjabi language skills and demonstrate their learning through SACE-aligned assessment tasks.

  • Who it is for: Students completing SACE who wish to include Punjabi in their learning plan
  • Focus areas: Reading, writing, speaking, listening, and cultural understanding
  • Learning approach: Structured language learning with interactive and culturally meaningful activities

Assessment & Learning

Students complete a range of assessment tasks aligned with SACE requirements. These tasks support language development and encourage students to communicate confidently in Punjabi.

  • Oral communication tasks (speaking and interaction)
  • Written tasks (texts and responses appropriate to level)
  • Understanding and interpreting Punjabi texts
  • Cultural learning connected to language use

Specific assessment details are provided to enrolled students and families.

Who is eligible to enrol in SACE Punjabi?

SACE Punjabi is suitable for students completing SACE who wish to include Punjabi as part of their senior secondary learning plan. Students may be background speakers, heritage learners, or learners with prior Punjabi study.

  • Stage 1 (2026): students typically in Year 11 (or as per their school learning plan)
  • Stage 2 (planned 2027): students typically in Year 12 (or as per their school learning plan)
  • Placement: may include a brief language proficiency check to confirm the most suitable pathway

What level of Punjabi is required for SACE Punjabi?

Senior secondary Punjabi requires students to work with language at a more formal and academic level. Students should have (or be ready to develop) a strong foundation in Punjabi grammar and vocabulary, and be able to apply senior secondary study skills such as: text analysis, text production (structured writing), research, and prepared oral communication.

What will students learn and be assessed on?

Students develop reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills alongside cultural learning connected to language use. Assessment typically includes a mix of oral and written tasks aligned with SACE expectations.

How much study time is expected outside class?

Like any senior secondary subject, students should expect regular home study (reading, writing drafts, vocabulary, and speaking practice). The amount varies depending on the student‚Äôs confidence level and goals.

What if a student already speaks Punjabi at home?

Home speaking is a strong foundation. Senior Punjabi study focuses on formal skills such as grammar accuracy, reading and interpreting texts, structured writing, and confident oral communication in more academic contexts.
