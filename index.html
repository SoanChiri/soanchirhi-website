About Us

üì∏ Early days of Soan Chirhi School

Soan Chiri School of Language & Culture was established in 2022 with the goal of providing a structured and immersive Punjabi language learning experience. Our school follows the Australian Curriculum for Languages and integrates cultural learning to support students in building a strong connection with their heritage.

We offer classes for students of all ages and backgrounds, focusing on reading, writing, speaking, listening, and cultural appreciation. Through a dynamic and interactive teaching approach, we aim to make learning Punjabi engaging, meaningful, and relevant to students growing up in Australia.

We believe that language is more than communication‚Äîit is a bridge to identity, history, and values. By learning Punjabi, students strengthen bilingual skills, deepen cultural understanding, and develop pride in their roots.

New in 2026: We are registered to deliver SACE Stage 1 Punjabi, providing senior secondary students with an accredited pathway to formally recognise their Punjabi language learning. We aim to expand our senior secondary offerings to include SACE Stage 2 Punjabi from 2027.

Mission

Our mission is to provide a nurturing, inclusive, and engaging learning environment where students develop proficiency in the Punjabi language while deepening their connection to Punjabi culture and heritage.

We are committed to preserving and promoting the Punjabi language among young learners by fostering a love for their mother tongue through structured learning, interactive activities, and meaningful cultural experiences.

Through a curriculum aligned with Australian language education standards, we support students in developing strong reading, writing, speaking, listening, and comprehension skills, enabling them to become confident bilingual communicators.

As a registered provider of SACE Stage 1 Punjabi, our mission includes supporting senior secondary students to access accredited Punjabi language study and achieve formal recognition for their learning as part of their SACE.

We aim to nurture a lifelong appreciation for Punjabi language, literature, history, and traditions, ensuring that future generations maintain a strong linguistic and cultural identity within the Australian context.

Vision

Our vision is to cultivate a vibrant and inclusive learning community where students embrace their Punjabi heritage with pride, confidence, and a strong sense of identity. Through quality language education, cultural immersion, and community engagement, we aim to empower future generations to remain connected to their roots.

We envision a future where Punjabi continues to thrive as a living language in Australia‚Äîvalued not only within families and communities, but also recognised within formal education pathways. By fostering appreciation for Punjabi literature, music, history, and cultural values, we seek to build a strong and enduring cultural foundation for our learners.

As a registered provider of SACE Stage 1 Punjabi, our vision includes supporting senior secondary students to achieve academic recognition for their bilingual skills and confidently use Punjabi in academic, social, and future professional contexts.

Looking ahead, we aspire to expand our senior secondary offerings to include SACE Stage 2 Punjabi, enabling students to pursue advanced Punjabi language study and complete their SACE with Punjabi as a recognised subject.

By working in partnership with families, educators, and the broader community, we aim to create a sustainable and forward-looking educational environment where Punjabi language and culture continue to flourish for generations to come.

Admission

Soan Chiri School of Language & Culture welcomes students of all proficiency levels who wish to learn Punjabi. Enrolments are managed through Community Language Schools South Australia (CLSSA) to ensure a consistent and transparent admissions process.

How to Apply

  • Intake Periods: January and July
  • Assessment: Brief language proficiency check (where required)
  • Placement: Based on age and language proficiency level

To enrol, please complete the official CLSSA online enrolment form. Once submitted, our team will review your application and contact you with further information.
